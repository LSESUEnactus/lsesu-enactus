Current Projects

Creative Cycle

Creative Cycle is a project which promotes up-cycling and provides vulnerable women in London with the technical and entrepreneurial skills to expand their businesses onto the online market.
In 2014, we held workshops and mentoring sessions to drastically improve the confidence and standard of living of 3 women from Eaves UK by allowing them to have a more stable source of income.

Continuing from our previous pilot programme, we engaged 3 new partner charities, and tailored our programme structure into an introductory and an intermediate stream.
The introductory stream is targeted at women who are still unsure of their future path, helping them gain clarity on either starting their own businesses or entering the workforce.
For our intermediate stream, we brought on board women who are already budding entrepreneurs, who were limited to selling on market stalls.
We taught the women how to expand their business outreach by bringing their products online, and developing their financial skill set through accounting exercises and bookkeeping.

Gates to Knowledge

Gates to Knowledge is a project aiming to allow a secular school for underprivileged minority children in New Delhi become financially sustainable.

It was set up in 2014 and the team provided a series of business proposals for the school. The team travelled to Delhi in August 2014 to conduct first stage of the project.

Hope Community

Hope Community is a project designed to help ex-convicts rebuild their lives by setting up their own communal businesses.

We're currently developing a social franchising model with Bounce Back, a charity and a social enterprise focused on training and employment of ex offenders. This coming year, we intend to provide pre- and post-franchise training, one-to-one mentoring, and resource development to Bounce Back's beneficiaries.

Inspire

Inspire is a project aimed at empowering underconfident youths through delivering courses on presentation and interviewing skills.

We've secured a partnership with Rush Croft Sports College to begin delivering a Presentation and Interviewing Skills course to students.
Our partnership will continue in September with a second batch of students.

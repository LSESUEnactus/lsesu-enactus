Current Projects

Creative Cycle



Creative Cycle is a project aimed at helping vulnerable women to secure financial independence. We do this through assessing their career goals, whether it be to become an entrepreneur or to get into paid employment. Based on this division, we offer them a series of workshops teaching them skills that are relevant to helping them to achieve their aims. The project is very much tailored to their individual needs and so we're very responsive to feedback.



Examples of workshops include teaching them online selling, marketing, accounting, sessions about performing well at work experience and customer service role plays. We are also partnered up with businesses to offer the women work experience.

We are interested in first and second year female students with the passion to help others, to become mentors, who are committed to the cause, have good communication skills, patience and empathy, and most importantly a willingness to learn and adapt to their mentees needs. No prior mentoring experience or work with vulnerable women is required, as we will train you! Contact us to find out more.

Gates to Knowledge



Gates to Knowledge is a social project that works with women within an underprivileged community in North East Delhi, India.

We aim to provide these women with an opportunity to engage in craft-making out of waste denim produced from local factories, in order to increase their financial independence. Thus far, the women have been producing denim iPhone cases for a month, which will then be sold by the factory in India. Looking forward, we hope to better equip these budding artisans with the tools to transition into small and growing business owners by implementing our financial literacy scheme and marketing and selling the cases here in London.

We are looking for 1 dedicated, innovative fresher to join us this year. Contact us to find out more!

Hope Community

Hope Community is a project designed to help ex-convicts rebuild their lives by setting up their own communal businesses.

We're currently developing a social franchising model with Bounce Back, a charity and a social enterprise focused on training and employment of ex offenders. This coming year, we intend to provide pre- and post-franchise training, one-to-one mentoring, and resource development to Bounce Back's beneficiaries.

Inspire

Inspire is a project aimed at empowering underconfident youths through delivering courses on presentation and interviewing skills.

This past year, we've secured a partnership with Rush Croft Sports College, a coeducational secondary school in London, to deliver presentation and interviewing Skills courses to students.
Our partnership will continue in September with a second batch of students, and a new round of courses that you can be involved in planning and executing.

New Chance

The team at New Chance works with homeless families to shape their lives by helping them create their own businesses.

Currently, we're moving towards a joint project with Westminster Befriend a Family (WBAF), who provides practical and emotional support to families with children and teenagers, and Cardinal Hume Community Centre, who support homeless young people, badly housed families and others in need, in gaining the skills they need to overcome poverty and homelessness. We will be conducting this project in September 2015. Contact us to find out more!
